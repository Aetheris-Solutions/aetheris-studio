Information

What the site and qualification form collect

Information you submit

The commerce qualification form asks for information needed to understand the business and the proposed work:

  • your name, work email address, role, company or brand name and store or company URL;
  • commerce platform, annual revenue band, monthly advertising-spend band and primary market;
  • relevant workstreams, the commercial problem, the event that triggered the enquiry and the desired timeline;
  • project-budget band, decision-making or sponsor readiness, and the constraint entered in the required free-text field;
  • the privacy-notice acknowledgement, the current wording version, and client and authoritative server receipt times;
  • a random submission identifier, deterministic qualification result, server receipt time and SHA-256 evidence hash used for idempotency and accountability.

Do not put payment-card data, health data, criminal-offence data, credentials, or confidential personal information about other people in a free-text field.

This release does not offer a marketing opt-in. The form sends a negative marketing state and the server rejects a crafted positive value; submitting an enquiry does not subscribe you to promotional communications.

Attribution and technical context

Only after you consent to analytics, the site may read and remember a first-touch attribution snapshot. The local snapshot may include UTM campaign values, advertising click identifiers such as GCLID, WBRAID or GBRAID, a sanitised landing path, a sanitised referrer and capture time. Recognised acquisition fields are preserved locally, while arbitrary query-string values and unrecognised fragments are removed before GTM, GA4 or Clarity is loaded. Stored landing and referrer URLs contain origin and path only.

With that consent, the first-touch snapshot is held for no more than 90 days in local storage under aetheris_studio_attribution_v1. It is removed when consent expires or is withdrawn. If you submit the form while analytics consent is still valid, the recognised campaign fields and sanitised URLs may be sent to Aetheris; the local capture timestamp is not included. The form works without attribution. See the cookies and browser storage notice for controls.

If analytics is enabled, Google Analytics 4 and Microsoft Clarity may receive technical and interaction information such as the page path, approximate location derived from the connection, device/browser characteristics, page and session events, clicks, scrolls and rendered-page interactions. The qualification event layer is designed to exclude names, email addresses, companies, store URLs, free text and CRM identifiers.

Cloudflare Pages and Cloudflare Turnstile may process request metadata, device or browser signals and an IP address to deliver the site and protect the form from abuse. The server forwards the connecting IP to Turnstile's verification service when it is available; the qualification ledger sent to Attio does not include that IP address.

Triage

How qualification and scoring work

Aetheris uses deterministic lead-qualification profiling to triage website requests. Fixed rules consider company scale, commerce readiness, primary market, scope and clarity of the problem, timing, stated project budget and access to a decision maker or sponsor. The result assigns an internal fit and priority level.

A high-fit result can reveal the option to continue to Aetheris Studio's Cal.com booking page. Other valid requests enter a review outcome and may be assessed manually. The public response is deliberately limited to qualified or review; it does not expose the score or internal reasons.

This triage does not accept or reject a contract and is not intended to produce a legal or similarly significant effect. Aetheris can review the underlying request and decide how to respond. If you want a person to reconsider the route applied to your request, contact us using the details above.

Because this routing is profiling, Aetheris will maintain a legitimate-interests assessment and a screening record for Article 22 GDPR before production. The statement above depends on the workflow remaining advisory: it must not autonomously conclude, refuse or price a contract, or otherwise create a legal or similarly significant effect.

Recipients

Where the information goes

Attio CRM

After validation and Turnstile verification, the server uses Attio as Aetheris's customer relationship management platform. It checks the unique Website Inbound submission identifier and adds the request beneath one pre-existing internal technical intake record. Submitted names, work emails, roles, company names and store URLs are stored on that list entry and in its evidence ledger; they are not used by the public form to query or create a Person, associate a submitted email domain with a Company, or overwrite canonical Person or Company fields.

Each accepted request is added as its own application-append-only Website Inbound list entry. That entry contains the unique submission identifier, authoritative server receipt time, deterministic SHA-256 payload hash, fit/priority and the complete normalised ledger, including qualification inputs, consent evidence, emailVerified: false and turnstileVerified: true. It excludes the Turnstile token, IP address, honeypot and secrets. Attio workspace administrators can still edit CRM data, so this is an accountable application convention, not a cryptographically immutable or WORM archive.

Attio Limited, 42 St John’s Square, 2nd Floor, London EC1M 4EA, United Kingdom, processes CRM customer data for Aetheris under its Data Processing Addendum. The UK benefits from an EU adequacy decision; onward transfers described by Attio are covered by an adequacy mechanism or contractual safeguards such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses, as applicable.

Cloudflare

Cloudflare provides Pages hosting, edge request handling and Turnstile abuse protection. The browser loads the Turnstile widget from Cloudflare and the server validates its short-lived token with Cloudflare before any CRM write.

Turnstile processes signals including IP address, TLS fingerprint, user-agent header, site key and origin. Cloudflare describes itself as Aetheris's processor when detecting bots for this site and as a separate controller when using signals to improve Turnstile. Details are in the Turnstile Privacy Addendum.

Stewardship

Retention and security

  • Unconverted enquiries and qualification ledgers: no more than 24 months after the last meaningful interaction, unless a longer period is needed for an active dispute or legal obligation. Low-fit leads should be reviewed for deletion after 12 months.
  • Active opportunities: while substantive discussions continue, then up to 24 months after the last meaningful interaction.
  • Clients: for the relationship and the period needed for contract administration and claims. Accounting and tax documents may be retained for 10 years where Italian law requires it; that period must not automatically be applied to free text and qualification ledgers.
  • Browser consent and attribution: a durable local consent choice lasts six months; if local storage is unavailable, its fallback lasts only for the current tab or browser session. First-touch attribution lasts no more than 90 days and is removed on expiry, rejection or withdrawal.
  • Analytics: the production GA4 property is to use no more than 14 months for user- and event-level data, with reset-on-activity disabled and first-party cookie lifetime configured to 13 months or less. Standard aggregated reports may remain longer. Clarity normally exposes recordings for 30 days; selected recordings and aggregate heatmap data may remain for up to nine months under Microsoft's service rules.
  • Security: Turnstile tokens are short-lived and normally expire within minutes. The production owner must verify Cloudflare Pages, edge and security-log retention against the account configuration and contract.

This schedule is the launch target. Until the automated deletion/review workflow is evidenced, general production collection remains blocked and preview submissions are restricted to explicitly authorised tests and manually reviewed enquiries; policy wording alone does not enforce deletion.

The implementation uses server-side credentials, encrypted hosting secrets, same-origin and hostname checks, payload limits, a honeypot, Turnstile verification and restricted CRM fields. No internet transmission or storage system is completely risk-free, and these measures do not constitute a guarantee of absolute security.
